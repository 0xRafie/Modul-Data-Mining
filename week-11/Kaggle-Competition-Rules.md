# Kaggle Competition Rules Draft

### 1. Competition Title

Data Mining Sains Data ITS - SVC [Week 11]

### 2. Competition Sponsor

Praktikum Data Mining - Sains Data ITS

### 3. Competition Sponsor Address

Institut Teknologi Sepuluh Nopember (ITS), Surabaya, Indonesia

### 4. Competition Website

https://www.kaggle.com/competitions/data-mining-sains-data-its-svc-week-11

No monetary prizes. This competition is intended for educational practice and class evaluation only.

### 6. Winner License Type

Non-Exclusive

### 7. Data Access and Use

Competition Use and Non-Commercial & Academic Research.

Participants may access and use the competition data for non-commercial purposes only, including participating in this competition, discussing solutions on Kaggle.com forums/class discussion channels, and academic learning or education. Redistribution outside the competition or course context is not permitted without permission from the competition host.

## Competition-Specific Rules

### 1. Team Limits

a. The maximum team size is five (5).

b. Team mergers are allowed before the team merger deadline if enabled in the Kaggle competition settings.

### 2. Submission Limits

a. Participants may submit a maximum of five (5) submissions per day.

b. Participants may select up to two (2) final submissions for judging.

### 3. Competition Timeline

Competition timeline dates, including start date, entry deadline, team merger deadline, and final submission deadline, are reflected on the competition's Overview > Timeline page.

### 4. Competition Data

#### a. Data Access and Use

Participants may access and use the Competition Data for non-commercial purposes only, including for participating in the Competition and on Kaggle.com forums/class discussion channels, and for academic research and education. The Competition Sponsor reserves the right to disqualify any participant who uses the Competition Data other than as permitted by the Competition Website and these Rules.

#### b. Data Security

Participants agree to use reasonable measures to prevent persons who have not formally agreed to these Rules from gaining access to the Competition Data. Participants agree not to transmit, duplicate, publish, redistribute, or otherwise make the Competition Data available to parties not participating in the Competition, except as permitted by the Competition Website and these Rules.

### 5. Winner License

Under the Winners Obligations section of the General Rules, each winner grants the Competition Sponsor a worldwide, non-exclusive, royalty-free right to use, reproduce, and display the winning submission, methodology, and code for educational and academic purposes related to the course.

Winners may be required to provide a short explanation of their methodology, including preprocessing, feature engineering, model configuration, validation strategy, hyperparameters, and inference/submission generation steps.

### 6. External Data and Tools

External data is not allowed unless explicitly approved by the competition host.

Participants may use open-source Python libraries and tools that are reasonably accessible to all participants at no cost, including but not limited to NumPy, pandas, scikit-learn, matplotlib, and Optuna.

Automated machine learning tools are allowed only if they are freely accessible to all participants and the participant can explain the resulting workflow. Participants are still responsible for understanding and explaining their submitted solution.

### 7. Eligibility

This competition is intended for students enrolled in or participating in the Praktikum Data Mining course. Course assistants, lecturers, and competition hosts may participate for testing purposes but are not eligible for leaderboard-based recognition unless explicitly stated by the course instructor.

### 8. Winner's Obligations

As a condition for leaderboard-based recognition or grading, selected participants may be required to submit:

1. Source code or notebook used to generate the final submission.
2. A short explanation of preprocessing, modeling, validation, and prediction steps.
3. Information about libraries, versions, and random seeds used.
4. Any additional files needed to reproduce the submitted predictions.

### 9. Governing Law

This educational competition follows Kaggle's general competition rules and the academic policies of Institut Teknologi Sepuluh Nopember (ITS). Any course-related disputes, grading decisions, or academic integrity issues will be handled by the course instructor and teaching assistant team according to applicable course policies.
